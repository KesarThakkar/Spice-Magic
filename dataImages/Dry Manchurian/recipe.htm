Dry Manchurian

Information:

Dry Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese dish that is typically made with vegetables like cauliflower and other vegetables, coated in a flavorful batter and stir-fried with spices. It is known for its crispy texture and tangy, spicy flavor. Dry Manchurian is a fusion dish that combines elements of Chinese cooking techniques and Indian flavors. It is believed to have originated in the Indian subcontinent and gained popularity as part of the Indo-Chinese cuisine. Dry Manchurian can be customized based on personal preferences and dietary restrictions. Variations may include using different vegetables, adding additional spices or seasonings, or adjusting the level of spiciness.Dry Manchurian is a popular dish in Indian restaurants and is enjoyed by many as a flavorful and satisfying appetizer or snack.

Ingredients:

For the vegetable balls:

1.      Mixed vegetables: Commonly used vegetables include finely chopped or grated carrots, cabbage, bell peppers (capsicum), and green beans. You can use a combination of vegetables based on your preference.

2.      All-purpose flour (maida)

3.      Cornstarch

4.      Salt

5.      Black pepper

6.      Ginger-garlic paste

7.      Soy sauce

8.      Water

 For the sauce:

1.      Oil (for stir-frying)

2.      Garlic cloves, minced

3.      Ginger, grated or minced

4.      Green chilies, chopped (optional)

5.      Onion, finely chopped

6.      Bell peppers (capsicum), thinly sliced

7.      Soy sauce

8.      Tomato ketchup

9.      Chili sauce

10.  Vinegar

11.  Salt

12.  Black pepper

13.  Spring onions (scallions), chopped (for garnish)

14.  Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)

These ingredients form the basic components for Vegetable Dry Manchurian. You can adjust the quantities of the vegetables and spices according to your taste and preferences. Additionally, you can add other vegetables like mushrooms, broccoli, or cauliflower to enhance the flavor and texture.

Instructions:

Here are instructions to prepare Vegetable Dry Manchurian:

  1. Prepare the vegetable balls:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine finely chopped or grated mixed vegetables (carrots, cabbage, bell peppers, green beans, etc.).
    • Add all-purpose flour (maida), cornstarch, salt, black pepper, ginger-garlic paste, and soy sauce to the vegetables.
    • Mix well to form a dough-like mixture. If needed, add a little water to bind the ingredients together.
    • Shape the mixture into small balls or desired shapes.
  2. Fry the vegetable balls:
    • Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai for deep frying.
    • Once the oil is hot, carefully add the vegetable balls into the oil.
    • Fry them on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crispy.
    • Remove the fried vegetable balls from the oil and place them on a kitchen towel to absorb excess oil.
  3. Prepare the sauce:
    • In a separate pan, heat some oil for stir-frying.
    • Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies (if desired). Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
    • Add finely chopped onions and thinly sliced bell peppers (capsicum). Stir-fry until the vegetables are slightly tender.
  4. Add sauces and seasonings:
    • Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, chili sauce, vinegar, salt, and black pepper to the stir-fried vegetables.
    • Mix well and cook for a few minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  5. Combine the vegetable balls with the sauce:
    • Add the fried vegetable balls to the sauce.
    • Gently toss the vegetable balls in the sauce until they are well coated.
  6. Garnish and serve:
    • Garnish the Vegetable Dry Manchurian with chopped spring onions (scallions) and fresh cilantro leaves.
    • Transfer the prepared Dry Manchurian to a serving dish.
    • Serve it hot as an appetizer or as a side dish with fried rice or noodles.
