Fried Rice

Information:

Veg fried rice is a beloved vegetarian dish that showcases the art of Chinese cuisine. Fragrant jasmine or basmati rice is stir-fried with an assortment of vibrant vegetables like carrots, peas, corn, mushrooms, and diced bell peppers. The vegetables are cooked until tender-crisp, while garlic, ginger, and soy sauce infuse the rice with a rich umami flavor. This colorful and nutritious dish is not only a standalone delight but also pairs well with other Chinese dishes. Veg fried rice offers a delicious and satisfying way to enjoy a plant-based meal.

Nutritional Information:

The nutritional information for Veg fried rice can vary depending on the specific ingredients and cooking methods used. However, here's a general overview of the potential nutritional components:

Calories: A typical serving of veg fried rice (approximately 1 cup) can contain around 200-300 calories, but this can vary based on portion size and ingredients.

  1. Carbohydrates: Rice is the primary source of carbohydrates in veg fried rice. A serving may provide around 30-40 grams of carbs, depending on the quantity of rice used.
  2. Protein: Without additional protein ingredients, veg fried rice may have a lower protein content, generally around 5-10 grams per serving.
  3. Fat: The fat content can be relatively low in veg fried rice if minimal oil is used for stir-frying. However, the presence of added oils or sauces can contribute to the overall fat content, typically around 5-10 grams per serving.
  4. Fiber: Vegetables in veg fried rice provide dietary fiber, offering several grams per serving. This can aid in digestion and promote a feeling of fullness.
  5. Vitamins and Minerals: The vegetable components in Chinese veg fried rice can provide various vitamins and minerals, including vitamins A, C, and K, as well as potassium, folate, and magnesium.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked rice (preferably chilled or day-old rice)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as diced carrots, peas, bell peppers, and corn)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped scallions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and diced onion to the pan and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until fragrant and slightly golden.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables to the pan and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until they are tender-crisp.
  4. Add the cooked rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps with a spatula. Stir-fry for a few minutes to heat the rice and combine it with the vegetables.
  5. Drizzle soy sauce and oyster sauce (if using) over the rice. Mix everything together, ensuring the rice is evenly coated with the sauces.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning or add more soy sauce if desired.
  7. Drizzle sesame oil over the rice and stir-fry for an additional minute to incorporate the flavors.
  8. Remove from heat and garnish with chopped scallions.
  9. Serve hot as a main course or as a side dish alongside your favorite Chinese dishes.
