Hakka Noodles

Information:

Hakka noodles are a beloved Chinese dish known for their thin, wheat-based noodles stir-fried with an array of vegetables and proteins. They are typically seasoned with garlic, ginger, and soy sauce, resulting in a savory and flavorful taste. Hakka noodles offer a delightful combination of textures, with the noodles being soft yet slightly chewy. This versatile dish is enjoyed by people worldwide as a delicious and satisfying meal that can be customized with various ingredients and spice levels.

Nutritional Information:

The nutritional information for Hakka noodles are:-

·         Calories: A typical serving of Hakka noodles (around 1 cup) may contain approximately 200-300 calories, depending on the ingredients and cooking methods.

·         Carbohydrates: Hakka noodles are primarily made from wheat flour, so they are relatively high in carbohydrates. A serving may contain around 40-60 grams of carbohydrates.

·         Fat: The fat content of Hakka noodles depends on the cooking oil used and any added oils or sauces. A serving may contain approximately 5-10 grams of fat.

·         Fiber: The fiber content in Hakka noodles is typically low, ranging from 1-3 grams per serving.

·         Sodium: Hakka noodles often contain soy sauce and other seasonings, which can contribute to sodium content. The amount of sodium can vary but may range from 500-1000 milligrams per serving.

Ingredients:

The ingredients used to prepare Hakka noodles are:-

  • 8 ounces of Hakka noodles or any thin egg noodles
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 cup of mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, cabbage, beans, etc.), thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of chili sauce (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped spring onions (scallions) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Hakka noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and rinse with cold water to prevent sticking. Set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pan. Sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the mixed vegetables to the pan and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they start to soften.
  5. Push the vegetables to one side of the pan and add the cooked noodles to the other side.
  6. Pour the soy sauce, oyster sauce, vinegar, and chili sauce (if using) over the noodles. Toss everything together to combine and coat the noodles with the sauce.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes until everything is well combined and heated through.
  8. Remove from heat and garnish with chopped spring onions (scallions).
  9. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious homemade Hakka noodles!

Feel free to adjust the ingredients and quantities according to your taste preferences and dietary requirements. You can also add additional sauces or spices to enhance the flavor to your liking.
